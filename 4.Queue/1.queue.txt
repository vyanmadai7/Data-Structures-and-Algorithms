-> A queue is a linear data structure that follows the FIFO (First In, First Out) principle,
meaning the element inserted first is removed first.
It works like a real life line of people waiting for service,
where the first person in line is served first. In a queue,
insertion (called enqueue) happens at the rear end, 
and deletion (called dequeue) happens at the front end. 
Queues are commonly used in task scheduling,
printer spooling, and handling requests in operating systems.
